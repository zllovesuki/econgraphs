Revealed Preference (4.4)

We don't observe utility functions directly.

we observe choices.

If bundles A and B are both affordable,

and a consumer chooses A,

we say that A is revealed preferred to B.

Time Value of Money (Ch. 4, Appendix 2)

We derived budget lines for two periods.

You can do it for more -- or even infinite periods

Two-Period Present Value Budget Constraint

\displaystyle{c_1 + \frac{c_2}{1+r} = I_1 + \frac{I_2}{1+r}}

T-Period Present Value Budget Constraint

\displaystyle{\sum_{t=1}^T\frac{c_t}{(1+r)^{t-1}} = \sum_{t=1}^T\frac{I_t}{(1+r)^{t-1}}}

Think about maximizing u(c_1, c_2,..., c_T)
subject to this constraint...

Economic Rent (9.5)

The portion of income
paid to a factor of production
in excess of that which is needed
to keep it in its current use.

Arises from (often artificial) scarcity (e.g., price controls).

Rent-seeking behavior: trying to make things more scarce (e.g., guilds, political corruption).

Interesting podcast: Why the British Drink Beer

Import Tariffs and Quotas (10.5)

Two types of firms: foreign and domestic

Tariff: tax on imported quantity

Quota: restriction on imported quantity

Multi-Plant Monopolies (11.4)

Production function split among different plants,
with different cost structures

Basically: equalize marginal cost of last unit produced at each plant.

Cartels (11.4)

A group of firms getting together to act like a monopolist

Restricting output helps them all, but each faces an incentive to produce more
