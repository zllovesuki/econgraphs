Cost Minimization

and Profit Maximization:

Two Sides of the Same Coin

The production function q = f(L,K)

maps quantities of inputs

like labor (L) and capital (K)

onto a quantity of output (q)

Given a production function \color{red}{q} = f(\color{green}{L,K})

input prices (w,r),

and the market price of output (P),

there are two ways of thinking about the firm's problem:

.

Profit

=

Total Revenue

-

Total Costs

Choosing inputs:

\pi(\color{green}{L,K})

=

P \times f(\color{green}{L,K})

-

(w\color{green}{L} + r\color{green}{K})

Choosing output:

\pi(\color{red}{q})

=

P \times \color{red}{q}

-

TC(\color{red}{q})
(Value of output) (Cost of inputs)

We want to find TC(\color{red}{q}), given current input prices

(the lowest possible cost of producing q units,
given wage rate w and rental rate of capital r)

.

Step 1:

Find cost-minimizing \color{green}{L^*}(w,r,\color{red}{q}) and \color{green}{K^*}(w,r,\color{red}{q}),

Step 2:

Plug back into expenditure function:

TC(w,r,\color{red}{q}) = w\color{green}{L^*}(w,r,\color{red}{q}) + r\color{green}{K^*}(w,r,\color{red}{q})
.

\color{green}{L^*}(w,r,\color{red}{q}) and \color{green}{K^*}(w,r,\color{red}{q}) are called the conditional demand
for labor and capital (i.e., conditional on \color{red}{q}).

Cost Minimization in Consumer Theory and Producer Theory

Consumer Theory

Producer Theory

\min_{x,y} P_xx + P_yy
s.t. u(x,y) = U
\min_{L,K} wL + rK
s.t. f(L,K) = q

Solve for optimal quantities; the solutions are:

Hicksian Demand Functions:

x^*(U,P_x,P_y), y^*(U,P_x,P_y)

Conditional Demand Functions:

L^*(w,r,q), K^*(w,r,q)

Plug optimal quantities back into the objective function:

Expenditure Function:

E(U,P_x,P_y) = P_xx^*(U,P_x,P_y) + P_yy^*(U, P_x,P_y)

(Cost of cost-minimizing choice)

Total Cost Function:

TC(w,r,q) = wL^*(w,r,q) + rK^*(w,r,q)

(Cost of cost-minimizing inputs)

Cost Minimization

when Calculus Works

If these three conditions hold:
(a) The production function is smooth,
marginal products are positive,
and isoquants are strictly convex
(b) The isoquants do not cross the axes
(c) The isocost lines are simple straight lines
Then the solution to
the cost-minimization problem
for q units of output:
1. Will involve strictly positive quantities L and K
(an interior solution, not a corner solution)
2. Can be found using the Lagrangian method
3. Is characterized by the conditions
\displaystyle{\frac{MP_L}{MP_K} = \frac{w}{r} }       and       f(K,L) = q

(i.e., is the point on the relevant isoquant that satisfies the tangency condition)
