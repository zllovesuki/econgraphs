Returns to scale

Suppose that if you use

L units of labor and K units of capital

you produce q = f(L,K) units of output.

If you use tL and tK for some t > 1,

is the amount you produce - that is, f(tL,tK) -

more or less than tq units of output?

(Colloquially: "If you double all inputs,
do you double your output, or produce more, or less?")

RETURNS TO SCALE: DEFINITIONS

Decreasing
returns to scale		 f(tL,tK) < tf(L,K) "Doubling all inputs produces
less than double the output."
Constant
returns to scale		 f(tL,tK) = tf(L,K) "Doubling all inputs produces
exactly double the output."
Increasing
returns to scale		 f(tL,tK) > tf(L,K) "Doubling all inputs produces
more than double the output."

Intuition: Amount of Y the consumer

New Concept: Elasticity of Substitution

\displaystyle{\sigma = \frac{\%\Delta \frac{K}{L}}{\%\Delta MRTS}}

Since it's more natural to think of the MRTS as a function of \frac{K}{L}...

\displaystyle{\sigma = \frac{1}{\left(\frac{\%\Delta MRTS}{\%\Delta \frac{K}{L}}\right)}}= \frac{1}{\left(\frac{d\ln MRTS}{d\ln \frac{K}{L}}\right)}

The CES Production Function

{% raw %}
f(L,K) = \left[L^\frac{\sigma - 1}{\sigma} + K^\frac{\sigma - 1}{\sigma}\right]^\frac{\sigma}{\sigma - 1}
