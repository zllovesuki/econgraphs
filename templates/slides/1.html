Econ 50

Marginal cost is the cost of producing an additional unit of output.

Marginal cost is the derivative of the total cost function, $TC(q)$.

Changes to fixed costs do not affect marginal costs.

$$TC(q) = FC + VC(q) \\ \frac{dTC(q)}{dq} = 0 + \frac{dVC(q)}{dq}$$

Econ 1

Econ 50

A competitive firm maximizes profits by setting price equal to...marginal cost.

$$P = MC$$

A competitive firm maximizes its profit function, which is:

$$\begin{align*}\pi(q) &= TR(q) - TC(q) \\ &= P \times q - TC(q)\end{align*}$$

To do this, it takes the derivative of the profit function: $$\frac{d\pi(q)}{dq} = P - \frac{dTC(q)}{dq}$$

Setting this equal to zero yields: $$P = \frac{dTC(q)}{dq}$$

Econ 50

The demand curve for a good shows the quantity demanded at each possible price of that good.

$$Q(P) = 10 - P$$

An agent with a given income will observe all prices in the market and choose a bundle of quantities.

$$\begin{align*}q_{x}^{*}(p_{x},p_{y},I) &= \frac{I}{p_{x}} \frac{1}{1 + \frac{p_{x}}{p_{y}}}\\ q_{y}^{*}(p_{x},p_{y},I) &= \frac{I}{p_{y}} \frac{1}{1 + \frac{p_{y}}{p_{x}}}\end{align*}$$

Changes to income and the
prices of other goods
will shift the demand curve.

Income and prices of other goods
are explicitly included
in the demand functions.
