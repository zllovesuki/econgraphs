Group Work: Budget Constraints and Optimal Choice

  1. Changes in a simple budget constraint

    Consider a simple budget constraint of the form P_xx+P_yy = I. Each member of the team should draw a diagram illustrating one of the following phenomena, and explain the reasoning to the rest of the team.

    1. An increase or decrease in income.
    2. An increase or decrease in the price of good X.
    3. An increase or decrease in the price of good Y.

  2. Comparing MRS and the price ratio (Winter 2015 Midterm Question)

    1. What does it mean if MRS_{x,y} < \frac{P_x}{P_y} at a point along a consumer's budget constraint?
    2. If a consumer is in a position where that is true, can they always improve their utility by changing their consumption bundle? Why or why not? Illustrate your answer with one or two carefully drawn budget-line/indifference-curve diagrams.
    3. If they could improve their utility by changing their consumption bundle, would it involve consuming more X and less Y, more Y and less X, or would it depend upon the exact form of the utility function in question? Carefully state the assumptions underlying your answer.

  3. The Canonical Econ 50 Problem

    Maximize the Cobb-Douglas utility function u(x,y) = \alpha \ln x + (1-\alpha) \ln y subject to the simple budget constraint P_xx+P_yy = I.

    1. Find the demand functions q^D_x(P_x,P_y,I) and q^D_y(P_x,P_y,I)
    2. Verbally describe the behavior these demand functions represent.
    3. Do Cobb-Douglas preferences represent consumers choosing between goods they view as complements, substitutes, or neither?
