Group Work: Supply and Demand

  1. Solving for equilibrium

    Suppose there are N_C identical consumers, each of whom spends \frac{1}{4} of their income on a good:

    q^D(P) = \frac{\frac{1}{4}I}{P}

    There are N_F identical firms, each of which has an individual supply curve given by

    q^S(P) = \frac{P}{w}

    where w is the wage rate.

    1. Write down the equations for the market supply Q^S(P,w,N_F) and market demand Q^D(P,I,N_C).
    2. Sketch the supply and demand curves for this market if N_C = 100, N_F = 36, I = 64, and w = 9.
      Indicate the equilibrium price and quantity in your sketch.
    3. Suppose the number of firms quadrupled, to 144. Find the new equilibrium price, and calculate the change in consumer surplus.
    4. Write P^E and Q^E as general functions of I, N_C, w, and N_F.
    5. In which of the exogenous variables is P^E increasing? What about Q^E?

  2. Describing demand

    Each of the following expressions gives an individual's demand for good X in terms of income, the price of that good (P_X), the price of another good (P_Y), and income (I).

    For each expression, identify whether two goods are complements, substitutes, or neither.

    Try to give a verbal description of the kind of consumer behavior each expression represents. (This isn't really possible for one of the four...but it is for the other three!)

    1. q^D_X(P_X,P_Y,I) = \frac{I}{P_X + P_Y}
    2. q^D_X(P_X,P_Y,I) = \frac{I}{2P_X}
    3. q^D_X(P_X,P_Y,I) = \frac{I}{P_X} \text{ if } P_X < P_Y \text{, otherwise 0}
    4. q^D_X(P_X,P_Y,I) = \left(\frac{P_Y}{P_X + P_Y}\right)\frac{I}{P_X}
