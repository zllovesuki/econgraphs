Group Work: Hicksian and Marshallian Demand, CV, and EV

Suppose your team has a utility function u(x,y) = x^\alpha y^{1 - \alpha}.

  1. Pick a random number for your team's value of \alpha.

    Don't make it 0, 0.5, or 1 please... :)

    Once you've selected a value, go to this graph and drag the \alpha slider to the value for your team. You'll use this diagram for the rest of the questions.

  2. Draw your Hicksian and Marshallian Demand curves for X.

    Draw your Marshallian demand curve, and indicate the points corresponding to bundles A (which is what you buy when P_x = 2) and C (which is what you buy when P_x = 4).

    Add the two Hicksian demand curves to this diagram that pass through the two points you've indicated. Add the points that correspond to bundles B and E. (Hint: one of these has the same price as A and the same utility as C, and the other has the opposite...)

  3. Calculate CV and EV

    1. Calculate the cost of the four bundles (A, B, C, and E) at the relevant prices.
    2. Calculate your Compensating Variation, which is the difference in cost of bundles B and C.
    3. Calculate your Equivalent Variation, which is the difference in cost of bundles A and E.

  4. How much are you hurt?

    The diagram shows a doubling of the price of X, from $2 to $4. How much would your team be willing to pay to avoid this price increase?

    Suppose I want you to be as happy as you were before the price increase. How much would I have to pay you?
