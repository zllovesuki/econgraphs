Group Work

  1. Monopoly Price Controls

    A monopoly has a cost structure TC(Q) = 22.5 + \frac{1}{2}Q^2. (That total cost includes the opportunity cost of leaving the industry.)

    The monopoly  faces the inverse demand curve P(Q) = 12 - Q.

    We found last time that this monopoly (which has MC(Q) = Q) would charge $8 and produce 4 units.

    Let's think of "total surplus" in this market being the monopoly's producer surplus plus consumer surplus.

    Now suppose a regulator, seeking to increase surplus in the market, imposes a price ceiling \overline P.

    1. Find consumer surplus and producer surplus in the absence of any price controls.
    2. Conditional on the monopoly continuing to produce, what level of \overline P would maximize total surplus? Sketch a graph showing the demand curve, MC curve, CS, and PS.
    3. Add to your graph a curve showing the monopoly's average cost. Would the monopolist choose to stay in business if it were regulated to charge no more than that price?
    4. Write the monopolist’s marginal revenue function as a function of the quantity it produces and the price ceiling; i.e., MR(Q | \overline P).
    5. Find the monopolist’s optimal choice Q^*(\overline P) and its profit \pi(\overline P)
    6. Given the monopolist's optimal choice, write an expression for the consumer surplus resulting from the price ceiling, CS(\overline P).
    7. What is the "optimal" price ceiling from society's point of view, given how the monopolist will choose to respond to a price ceiling (potentially leaving the market)?

  2. Equilibrium with Taxes

    Suppose supply is given by Q^S(P_F) = P_F^2 and demand is given by Q^D(P_C) = \frac{1000}{P_C}.

    1. What is the price elasticity of demand? The price elasticity of supply?
    2. What is the equilibrium price and quantity in this market if there are no taxes or subsidies (so P_F = P_C = P)?
    3. Suppose the government imposes a 16% tax on this good, so P_C = 1.16P_F. Find the new equilibrium quantity, as well as P_C and P_F. (You can round your prices to the nearest nickel, and your quantity to the nearest integer, if you like.) Draw a graph showing the new equilibrium, with CS, PS, government revenue, and deadweight loss.
    4. How do the prices paid by consumers and firms in the presence of the tax relate to the elasticity of demand and supply?
