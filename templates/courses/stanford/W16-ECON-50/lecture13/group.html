Group Work: Short-run and Long-run production decisions

Suppose w = r = 10 and f(L,K)=L^\frac{1}{4}K^\frac{1}{4}.

  1. Assign an amount of capital to each person at the table: \overline{K} = 9, 16, 25\text{ or } 36 .
  2. Derive an expression for each person's labor requirements and total cost of producing q units.
  3. Using that expression, calculate each person's labor requirements and total cost of producing q=3,4,5,\text{ or }6 units.
  4. Create a table showing total cost of producing q=3,4,5,\text{ and }6 units for \overline{K} = 9, 16, 25\text{ or } 36 .
  5. Use this table to plot the four short-run total cost curves corresponding to the four levels of capital (\overline{K}). What is the Y-intercept of each curve?
  6. For each level of ouptut, which is the optimal level of capital?
  7. Plot the long-run total cost curve if you can choose the level of capital freely, depending on how much you want to produce. What is the Y-intercept of this curve?
