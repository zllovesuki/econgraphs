January 14

Group Work: Preferences

  1. Completeness and transitivity (Varian Ch. 3, Review Question 2)

    Consider a group of people A, B, C and the relation "at least as tall as," as in "A is at least as tall as B." Is this relation transitive? Is it complete?

  2. Drawing indifference maps

    Draw an indifference map for each of the following pairs of goods.

    1. Kittens and terrorist attacks (assuming kittens are good and terrorist attacks are bad)
    2. Ebola cases and terrorist attacks (assuming both of these things are bad)
    3. Nickels and dimes
    4. Nickels and quarters
    5. Hamburgers per day and french fries per day (make each axis go to 20.)
    6. Kale and bacon, for a vegetarian who just throws away any bacon they get

  3. Indifference curve facts

    Can two indifference curves cross? Why or why not?

    Can indifference curves be "thick"? That is, can a whole region be considered indifferent? Why or why not?

  4. Marginal Rate of Substitution

    What is the MRS of the indifference curves in question 2(c) and 2(d)?

    Suppose that "good Y" is "money spent on all other goods except good X." In this interpretation, what is the value of the MRS?
