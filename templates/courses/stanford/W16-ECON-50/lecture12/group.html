Group Work: Production Functions

Your group is making the production decisions for a firm with Cobb-Douglas production function q = L^a K^\frac{1}{2}. For each of the following questions, enter the numbers on the spreadsheet here.

  1. Choose your production function

    For your group, let a be the fraction of your group that has a birthday before July 1 (minimum 0.25), and let K be the number of people in your group who have a birthday after July 1 (minimum 1).

    Plug these values of a and K into your production function; for example, if one-third of your group was born before July 1, and 4 members of your group were born after July 1, then your production function would be q = L^a K^\frac{1}{2} = L^\frac{1}{3} \times 4^\frac{1}{2}.

  2. Do some initial calculations

    • Calculate your total output if L = 10, L = 20, and L = 30.
    • Calculate the marginal product of labor (MP_L) for the 10th, 20th, and 30th worker.
    • Is the MP_L diminishing?

  3. Returns to scale

    • Calculate your total output if L = 20, and you double your capital K. Compare this to your total output if L = 10 with your original level of capital.
    • Does your firm have constant, increasing, or decreasing returns to scale?

  4. Technological progress

    (For these questions, assume your level of capital is back to your initial level.)

    • Calculate your output when L = K (for your level of capital).
    • Calculate the MRTS_{L,K} when L = K (for your level of capital).
    • Calculate the percentage change in MRTS_{L,K} for a 1% change in the K/L ratio; confirm that this is 1.
    • Use desmos.com to draw an isoquant for the quantity you found above.
    • In Desmos, add another isoquant for the same quantity if you double your value of a. What happens?
    • Calculate your MRTS_{L,K} at the new (doubled) level of \alpha if you continue to produce the same quantity, and keep your capital/labor ratio constant (at 1).
